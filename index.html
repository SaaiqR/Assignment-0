Who was Marcus Aurelius? Marcus Aurelius si the father of the philophy called stoicism. Aurelius was born in 121 CE in Rome, and his family was in an authoritative position in Rome. Marcus was adopted by Hadrian to suceed him as emperor, and he became emperor of Rome when he turrned 40. What Marcus Aurelius is most widely known for though, is his philosophy. He wrote a series of texts called Meditations, in which he encourages virtue is the only good for oneself, vice is the only evil, and everything else is indifferentas far as ones happiness is concerned. He encourages the four virtues of stoicism: wisdom, temperance, justice, and courage. He believed that through any hardship humans face, they must not show signs of frustration or anger, and instead know that it has no effect on their happiness.
